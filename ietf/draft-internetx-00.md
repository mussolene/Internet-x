# Internet Draft: Internet-X

## Abstract
This document provides an overview of Internet-X, a novel architecture designed for next-generation internet applications. It emphasizes the importance of secure communication, identity management, and compatibility with post-quantum cryptography.

## Status of This Memo
This memo provides information for the Internet community. It does not specify an Internet standard of any kind.

## Conventions and Definitions
Terms used in this document are defined in the context of the IETF Internet-Draft specifications. In particular, "Internet-X" refers to the framework and protocols defined herein.

## Introduction
The evolution of internet technologies demands new frameworks to address emerging challenges such as security threats, identity management, and the need for mobility in a diverse environment.

## Architecture Overview
The architecture of Internet-X is designed to support efficient data dissemination and real-time communication among various internet-enabled devices while ensuring security and compliance with emerging standards.

## Identity Model
An identity model is defined that emphasizes the importance of secure and verifiable identity for participants in Internet-X. The model incorporates principles from existing identity frameworks but is tailored for next-generation applications.

## Packet Format
The packet format specified for Internet-X is streamlined to support various data types and enhance transmission efficiency while maintaining backward compatibility with legacy protocols.

## Handshake
A handshake protocol is defined to establish secure connections between participants in Internet-X, ensuring the mutual authentication of entities before any sensitive data is transmitted.

## Hybrid Post-Quantum Operation
This section discusses the potential impact of quantum computing on internet security and presents hybrid cryptographic operations that integrate classical and post-quantum algorithms to safeguard communications.

## Mobility
The mobility aspect of Internet-X addresses the challenges of devices transitioning across different networks, ensuring seamless connectivity and communication without interruption.

## Security Considerations
Security considerations are paramount in the design of Internet-X, addressing threats such as data interception, identity spoofing, and ensuring end-to-end encryption throughout the communication process.
